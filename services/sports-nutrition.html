The Science of Sports Nutrition

Why What You Eat Determines What You Achieve

Training breaks your body down. Nutrition is what builds it back up — stronger, faster, and more resilient. Without the right nutritional foundation, even the best training programme delivers a fraction of its potential results.

Sports nutrition isn't just about protein. It's about the precise combination of macronutrients, timed to your training, calibrated to your sport's specific demands, and executed with foods that your body recognises and uses efficiently.

"The athletes who plateau are almost always under-eating, over-restricting one macronutrient, or timing their nutrition incorrectly. Fix those three things and performance follows."

At Diet_Solver, we build your sports nutrition plan around your training type, training frequency, competition schedule, body composition goals, and the Indian foods you actually enjoy eating.

Macronutrient Strategy

The 3 Macros — and How We Use Them

Protein — The Builder

Repairs muscle fibres after training, stimulates growth, and supports immune function. Timing around workouts is critical for maximising synthesis.

🥚 Eggs · 🧀 Paneer · 🫘 Dal · 🐔 Chicken · 🌱 Tofu · 🥛 Curd
1.6–2.2g
per kg body wt

Carbohydrates — The Fuel

Your body's primary energy source for high-intensity exercise. Carb timing and type determine whether you have energy to train hard and recover fast.

🍚 Rice · 🫓 Roti · 🌽 Sweet potato · 🌾 Oats · 🍌 Banana · 🍠 Rajma
4–7g
per kg body wt

Healthy Fats — The Regulator

Supports hormone production, reduces exercise-induced inflammation, and provides sustained low-intensity energy. Never eliminate fats from an athlete's diet.

🥜 Nuts · 🌻 Seeds · 🫒 Olive oil · 🐟 Fish · 🥑 Avocado · 🧈 Ghee
20–35%
of total calories
Nutrient Timing Strategy

When You Eat Matters as Much as What You Eat

Nutrient timing is one of the most powerful — and most neglected — tools in sports nutrition. We map every meal to your training window.

2–3 Hours Before

Pre-Workout Fuel

Complex carbs + moderate protein + low fat. Tops up glycogen stores without causing sluggishness or GI discomfort during training.

🍚 Rice + dal + sabzi, or 🫓 Roti + paneer + vegetables
30–60 Min Before

Immediate Pre-Workout

Fast-digesting carbs + light protein. Provides a quick energy top-up and primes the anabolic window without weighing you down.

🍌 Banana + 5 soaked almonds, or 🥛 Milk + dates
During (60+ min)

Intra-Workout Fuelling

For sessions exceeding 60 minutes, strategic carbohydrate intake prevents glycogen depletion and maintains intensity throughout.

💧 Electrolyte water + 🍌 banana (if needed)
Within 30–45 Min

Post-Workout Recovery

Fast protein + moderate carbs immediately after training. Closes the anabolic window, jumpstarts muscle repair, and replenishes glycogen.

🥚 Eggs + 🍞 toast, or 🥛 Curd + 🍌 banana + 1 tsp honey

Sample Meal Plan

A High-Training-Day Eating Plan

Real Indian sports nutrition food — high-protein, performance-optimised, and genuinely satisfying. Shown for a gym/strength athlete on a heavy training day.

This is an illustrative sample for a strength athlete. Your plan will be completely personalised to your sport, body weight, training volume, and specific goals.
Training Day — Strength Athlete (approx. 80kg)
Early Morning 5 soaked almonds + 2 walnuts + 1 glass warm lemon water
Breakfast 4 egg whites + 1 whole egg omelette with spinach & onion + 2 multigrain rotis + 1 cup low-fat curd
Pre-Workout 1 banana + 1 cup milk with 1 tsp honey (30–45 min before training)
Post-Workout 2 boiled eggs + 1 cup curd + 1 banana (within 30 min of finishing)
Lunch 200g grilled chicken or 150g paneer + 1.5 cups rice + 1 cup dal + mixed vegetable sabzi + salad
Snack 1 cup roasted chana + 1 cup buttermilk (chaas)
Dinner 150g fish or chicken + 2 rotis + 1 cup dal + 1 cup vegetable sabzi
Before Bed 1 cup warm low-fat milk with a pinch of turmeric
Rest / Recovery Day — Same Athlete
Early Morning 5 soaked almonds + 1 glass warm lemon water
Breakfast 3 egg whites + 1 whole egg omelette + 1 multigrain roti + 1 cup curd + 1 seasonal fruit
Mid-Morning 1 cup green tea + small handful of mixed nuts (almonds, peanuts)
Lunch 150g chicken or paneer + 1 cup rice (reduced) + 1 cup rajma or chana dal + sabzi + salad
Snack 1 cup sprouted moong salad with lemon & chaat masala + 1 cup buttermilk
Dinner 150g grilled fish or 100g paneer + 1–2 rotis + 1 cup dal + 2 cups mixed vegetables
Before Bed 1 cup warm turmeric milk for overnight muscle repair
Competition / Race Day — Endurance Athlete
3 Hrs Before 1.5 cups rice + 1 cup dal + light vegetable sabzi (low fat & fibre — easy digestion)
60 Min Before 1 ripe banana + 4–5 dates + 200ml coconut water (quick carbs + electrolytes)
During Race Electrolyte water sips every 15–20 min + 1 banana at midpoint (sessions over 60 min)
Immediately After 200ml coconut water + 2 boiled eggs + 1 banana (within 20 min of finishing)
Recovery Meal 1.5 cups rice + 1 cup dal + 150g chicken or paneer + 1 cup curd
Evening Light khichdi with ghee + 1 cup curd + seasonal fruit (easy digestion, glycogen restock)
Before Bed 1 cup warm milk with turmeric + 1 tsp honey for overnight muscle repair
